What's a
screen reader?

Assistive software that
reads digital content and interfaces

But! We need to understand what on earth is a screen reader??

True to its name, it's an assistive technology, like my glasses.

[Next] It's a software that reads digital content and interfaces. It helps users who cannot look at the interface, for whatever reason, access other software and its content.

I'm emphasising on 'software that lets you access other software' here.

As web creators, it's often easy to forget that assistive technologies aren't solely made for accessing the web.

It's made so that users can access anything on their device. Installed apps and etc. Your web product just happen to be run on an app called a browser, something that assistive tool like screen reader could be used on.

Who use it?

  • Blind
  • Neurodiverse
  • AI assistant
  • Lazy ol' me

When I first learnt about screen readers, I thought only those who are completely blind uses it.

When I heard the word 'blind', I immediately visualised people who use a guide dog or a stick to move around.

Oh... how wrong I was. There are so many different access needs that is helped by screen readers. Here are a few that I'm aware of

  1. [Next] As I mentioned, many blind users use screen readers. But it's important to note that people can be 'blind' in different ways.

    There is a spectrum!

    Some people are fully blind from birth. Some lose their sight afterwards. Accidents and eye disease can happen to anyone.

    Some are not completely blind, but are 'legally' blind, which means they have partial use of their sight. Can still see shapes and forms, but perhaps not well enough to drive without glasses.

    Some of these legally blind users could also have different visual impairments like tunnel vision, where they can only see through a tiny gap, as if someone blocked parts of their eyes.

  2. [Next] Perhaps not as common. But neurodiverse users, which could include people with ADHD, Autism, and other cognitively diverse attributes, also use screen reader to help consume information.

    If you're wondering how... Have you ever been read to, or joined reading class at school? There's benefit in providing multiple ways to consume the same information. Especially when learning.

  3. [Next] AI assistant users are pretty much general high tech population.

    This may come as a surprise to you, but tools like Alexa, Siri, Google home essentially functions as screen reader when you ask it to look things up and read out to you. It's reading information from an app or a website to users.

Classic curb cut case, where benefits of designing for access needs like completely blind users also extend to other user groups.
