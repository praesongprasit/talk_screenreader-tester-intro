To keep things concise, I'm going to refer to Developers 'Dev'.

UX researcher? Designer? Devs in the room?

Why?

Create empathy and add context

UX spend all the time empathising with customers, yet not to their own devs

Last job, I worked in a service design agency. Seen difference when devs were and were not involved early

As a dev, I've grown. Changed the way I approach a problem

Once we've experienced this, if done right, the benefit is undeniable

Lately feels far away from my users. Given task, and no real user context

When?

How early should devs be involved? Depends

What kind of project? Is it a technical solution? Or is it a brochureware site?

Problem statements can be shared early

User research sessions can have devs join in

Data intensive projects can benefit from Dev wireframing. Yes UXers can do it with Axure, but devs don't have time to think about data models

How?

  • Ask
  • Align test session with appropriate dev
  • Roster devs
  • Turn them into observers and/or note taker
  • Share findings with the team

A lot of what I taked about means double budget (dev + design) early, which pays off

Some devs, especially BEDs, see no point in this. FEDs are often on board

When companies first try, they make devs sit in every testing session, both user and usability test. Pointless, deminishing return

Data heavy prototype, get devs to sit in first session, fix, and followup

Final synthesised findings give devs idea of how decisions are reached, important to them and not

Why not simply share finding?

It's an investment in your developers, and the team

Only sharing findings is like telling GoT beginning, and explain the ending

A dev who see users right click, will remember to markup links correctly

Small dev decisions are made without UX knowing. Database structure, allow scroll in iOS, how iframe gets put together and etc.

A dev who have witness his/her take 10 seconds to load during a session, is more likely to be on board with performance projects

Remind eachother of the experiences we've shared

UX is a journey

Best experience for developers
=
Best experience for users

What next?

UXer, go talk to your devs
Devs, ask to be involved

Homework!

UXer, find a dev. Dev, find UXer. Now talk about how you'd like to work with eachother

We've turned UI into UX Designers through involvement in research. Lets Devs into UX Devs too
